These dynamics make VNM an ideal subject for financial ratio analysis, allowing me to dissect how a legacy market leader optimizes its capital allocation strategy, manages working capital cycles, and balances dividend payouts against capital expenditures (CapEx) for international expansion.

## 4. Preliminary Observations & Hypotheses
Based on initial market tracking and preliminary reviews of VNM's corporate updates, I establish three directional and falsifiable hypotheses for the ratio modeling stage:
* **Hypothesis 1 (Profitability & Efficiencies):** I expect VNM’s net profit margin to exhibit a gradual compression from FY2023 to FY2025 because rising global inflation and supply chain disruptions have inflated raw material import costs faster than the company could adjust its retail pricing.
* **Hypothesis 2 (Liquidity & Working Capital):** I expect the Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO) and the overall Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) to lengthen over the analyzed period because VNM expanded its distribution infrastructure and held higher safety stocks to mitigate cross-border logistics risks.
* **Hypothesis 3 (Leverage & Capital Structure):** I expect a rising total debt ratio from FY2024 to FY2025 because the company utilizes low-cost corporate debt to finance its high-tech dairy farm expansions in domestic and regional markets rather than diluting equity.

## 5. Ratio Categories Preview
Given the capital-intensive nature of supply chain logistics and manufacturing in the FMCG industry, the most critical ratio categories for Vinamilk are:
* **Profitability Ratios (DuPont System):** Necessary to evaluate how gross margin strains filter down to Return on Equity (ROE).
* **Efficiency/Activity Ratios:** Critical for monitoring inventory turnover and receivable cycles, which dictate free cash flow generation.
* **Leverage Ratios:** Vital to understand VNM's debt capacity and how its capital structure choices protect its historic AAA-grade financial stability.
